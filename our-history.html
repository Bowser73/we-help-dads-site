Our History

From Big Brother volunteering to a comprehensive program serving hundreds of dads, the story of We Help Dads is one of commitment, growth, and community.

Founder's Journey

From Big Brother to We Help Dads

Our founder, Jeff Hutter, began volunteering as a big brother in the Kansas City Big Brother Big Sister program in 1975, shortly after graduating from the University of Kansas. He continued that role after moving to Louisville in the late 1970s, and then broadened his work with fatherless kids through his involvement with the Boys & Girls Clubs of Louisville.

Upon retiring in 2019, Mr. Hutter said, "It hit me, what if I worked to get estranged dads back into their kids' lives as opposed to trying to fill the father role myself." His early search for putting that approach to work led him to the then assistant Prosecuting Attorney in Madison County, Steve Koester—now Judge of the Family Court.

"Judge Koester walked me down the hall to Judge Angela Sims and staff director Katie Stapleton of Problem Solving Court, and from there our fatherhood program began."

Growth and Expansion

Since then, We Help Dads has provided class training to more than 200 troubled dads coming out of the court system in Madison County, and has been providing fatherhood instruction in the Hamilton County Jail through its partnership with the TOWER Program. This year, we began conducting classes in the Madison County Corrections Complex.

Beginning in 2024, We Help Dads and its volunteer graduate dads began providing mentorship to dads who have turned their lives around and need help in overcoming life obstacles that impede their role as a fully involved dad.
